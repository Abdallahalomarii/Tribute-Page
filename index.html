Dr.Verghese Kurien

Father of the White Revolution

Known as the "Father of the White Revolution" in India, was a social entrepreneur whose "billion-litre idea", Operation Flood.

Dr.Verghese Kurien life:

  • 1921 - Verghese Kurien is born in Calicut, British India.
  • 1940 - Kurien graduates in Physics from Loyola College, Chennai.
  • 1947 - Kurien completes his Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from the College of Engineering, Guindy.
  • 1949 - Kurien moves to the United States to pursue a Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Michigan State University.
  • 1950 - Kurien starts working at the National Dairy Development Board (NDDB) in India.
  • 1965 - Kurien founds the Gujarat Co-operative Milk Marketing Federation (GCMMF), which later launches the brand Amul.
  • 1973 - Kurien receives the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Community Leadership.
  • 1989 - Kurien is awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award.
  • 2012 - Kurien passes away at the age of 90 in Nadiad, Gujarat.

"His most notable achievement was the establishment of the Amul cooperative in Gujarat, which became the largest milk cooperative in the world and a model for similar initiatives in other developing countries. Kurien received numerous awards for his contributions, including the Padma Vibhushan, the Ramon Magsaysay Award, and the World Food Prize."
